7:T417,<h1>Building with constraints</h1>
<p>Constraints are often seen as obstacles. But I've found them to be catalysts—forcing creativity where abundance might have bred complacency.</p>
<h2>The 10KB challenge</h2>
<p>Recently, I challenged myself to build a complete landing page under 10KB. No frameworks, no dependencies. Just HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript.</p>
<p>The result wasn't just smaller—it was better. Every byte mattered. Every choice was intentional.</p>
<h2>Why constraints work</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Focus</strong>: Limited options force clear priorities</li>
<li><strong>Creativity</strong>: Boundaries inspire novel solutions</li>
<li><strong>Performance</strong>: Less code means faster experiences</li>
</ol>
<p>When you can't do everything, you learn to do the right things exceptionally well.</p>
<h2>Embrace the limitation</h2>
<p>The next time you face a constraint—whether it's time, budget, or technical—don't fight it. Lean into it. Let it guide you toward simplicity.</p>
<p>That's where the magic happens.</p>
