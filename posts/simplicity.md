# On the nature of simplicity

There's a peculiar beauty in constraints. When we strip away the unnecessary, what remains is essence—pure, unadorned, and often more powerful than what we started with.

## The paradox of choice

In design, as in life, we're often tempted to add. Another feature, another color, another layer of complexity. But true elegance emerges from subtraction, not addition.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

## Finding the essential

The challenge isn't in knowing what to keep—it's in having the courage to remove everything else. Every element should earn its place. Every word should carry weight.

When we embrace simplicity, we create space. Space for thought, for breath, for the user to find their own meaning in what we've created.
